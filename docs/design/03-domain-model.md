# 03 — Domain Model

This document describes the **domain model** for the Warehouse Management System (WMS).
The domain model focuses on the core business concepts, how they relate to each other, and
the rules that must always be enforced by the system.

The purpose of this model is to ensure that business logic is clearly defined and kept
separate from the user interface, API, and database concerns. This supports a clean,
maintainable design and allows the system to be extended later if required.

---

## 1. Domain Model Overview

The domain model is split into four main areas:

1. **Suppliers and Purchasing**  managing suppliers, purchase orders, and deliveries  
2. **Inventory** tracking products, stock levels, and stock movements  
3. **Customer Orders**  processing outgoing orders and reducing stock  
4. **Finance**  recording income and expenses and generating reports  

The model follows a lightweight Domain-Driven Design (DDD) approach, using entities,
value objects, and aggregates to keep related data consistent and enforce business rules.

---

## 2. Ubiquitous Language

To avoid ambiguity, the following terms are used consistently throughout the system:

| Term | Meaning |
|-----|--------|
| Product | An item stocked and sold by the warehouse |
| Supplier | A business the warehouse purchases products from |
| Purchase Order | An order placed with a supplier to restock inventory |
| Goods Receipt | A record confirming that stock has been received |
| Stock Movement | A record of stock increasing or decreasing |
| Customer Order | A record of products sold to a customer |
| Financial Transaction | An income or expense created by a sale or purchase |
| User Role | The role selected to access system operations (Warehouse Staff, Manager, Administrator) |

Note: In the initial codebase, **Product** is implemented as `StockItem`. The concept is the same.

---

## 3. Domain Entities

Entities have a unique identity and represent core business objects.

### Supplier
**Purpose:**  
Stores supplier details and links to purchase orders.

**Key data:**
- SupplierId
- Name
- ContactDetails

**Notes:**  
A supplier can have many purchase orders.

---

### User
**Purpose:**  
Represents the active system user and their role. This supports role-based workflows but does not include authentication.

**Key data:**
- UserId
- DisplayName
- Role (Warehouse Staff, Manager, Administrator)

**Notes:**  
Only one active user is assumed at a time (see system assumptions).

---

### Product
**Purpose:**  
Represents an item held in inventory and enforces stock rules.

**Key data:**
- ProductId
- SKU
- Name
- ReorderLevel
- UnitCost

**Key behaviour:**
- Increase stock
- Decrease stock (must not allow negative values)

---

### PurchaseOrder (Aggregate Root)
**Purpose:**  
Represents an order placed with a supplier and controls its lifecycle.

**Key data:**
- PurchaseOrderId
- SupplierId
- Status
- CreatedAt

**Key behaviour:**
- Add product lines
- Move through order statuses (Pending → Partially Received → Completed)
- Accept goods receipts
- Cancel order (Pending or Partially Received only)

A purchase order owns its order lines and is responsible for keeping them consistent.

---

### PurchaseOrderLine
**Purpose:**  
Represents a single product and quantity within a purchase order.

**Key data:**
- ProductId
- QuantityOrdered
- UnitCostAtOrder

---

### GoodsReceipt
**Purpose:**  
Provides an auditable record that goods were received for a purchase order.
This supports delivery tracking and order history requirements.

**Key data:**
- GoodsReceiptId
- PurchaseOrderId
- ReceivedAt

---

### GoodsReceiptLine
**Purpose:**  
Stores the quantity of a specific product received in a delivery.

**Key data:**
- ProductId
- QuantityReceived

---

### Customer
**Purpose:**  
Minimal customer record to support order tracking.

**Key data:**
- CustomerId
- Name

---

### CustomerOrder (Aggregate Root)
**Purpose:**  
Represents an outgoing customer order and controls order confirmation.

**Key data:**
- CustomerOrderId
- CustomerId
- Status
- CreatedAt

**Key behaviour:**
- Add order lines
- Confirm order (only if stock is available)
- Cancel order (Draft or Confirmed only)

---

### CustomerOrderLine
**Purpose:**  
Represents a product and quantity within a customer order.

**Key data:**
- ProductId
- Quantity
- UnitPriceAtSale

---

### StockMovement
**Purpose:**  
Acts as an immutable audit log of all stock changes.

**Key data:**
- StockMovementId
- ProductId
- Type (Receipt, Issue, Adjustment)
- Quantity
- OccurredAt
- Reason (for adjustments)
- ReferenceType
- ReferenceId

---

### FinancialTransaction
**Purpose:**  
Records income and expenses generated by sales and purchases.

**Key data:**
- TransactionId
- Type (Sale or PurchaseExpense)
- Amount
- Status (Pending, Posted, Voided, Reversed)
- OccurredAt
- ReferenceType
- ReferenceId
- ReversalOfTransactionId (optional)

---

### ReportExport
**Purpose:**  
Records when financial reports are exported and in what format.

**Key data:**
- ReportExportId
- ReportType
- Format (TXT or JSON)
- GeneratedAt
- FilePath
- DateRange (optional)

---

## 4. Value Objects

Value objects are immutable and validated.

### Money
- Amount (decimal)
- Currency (fixed to GBP)

### ContactDetails
- Email
- Phone
- Address (optional)

### DateRange
- From date
- To date

---

## 5. Aggregates and Boundaries

The system uses aggregates to keep related data consistent:

- **PurchaseOrder** is the root for purchase order lines and goods receipts  
- **CustomerOrder** is the root for customer order lines  

Only the aggregate root can be modified directly from outside the domain.

---

## 6. Key Business Rules

### Inventory
- Stock levels must never be negative
- Stock quantities must be whole numbers
- Low stock is triggered when stock is at or below the reorder level
- Stock adjustments must record a reason
- Adjustment movements may be negative to indicate decreases

### Purchasing
- Purchase orders must contain at least one line
- Ordered and received quantities must be greater than zero
- Received quantities must not exceed ordered quantities
- Purchase order status progresses through Pending → Partially Received → Completed
- Purchase orders can be cancelled only while Pending or Partially Received

### Customer Orders
- Orders must contain at least one line
- Orders cannot be confirmed if stock is insufficient
- Orders can be cancelled while Draft or Confirmed

### Finance
- All transactions must reference a purchase order or customer order
- Transaction amounts must be positive
- Currency is fixed to GBP
- Purchase orders create a Pending expense; receipts post the expense for received items
- Transactions may be voided or reversed; voided transactions are excluded from reports and reversals link to the original transaction

## 7. Repository Interfaces

Repositories abstract persistence from the domain model and allow the database
implementation to change without affecting business logic.

Planned repositories include:
- ISupplierRepository
- IProductRepository
- IPurchaseOrderRepository
- IGoodsReceiptRepository
- ICustomerOrderRepository
- IStockMovementRepository
- ITransactionRepository
- IReportExportRepository
